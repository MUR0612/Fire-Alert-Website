Preparation tips

Wildfires can spread quickly and unpredictably. Staying ahead of the flames means equipping yourself with the right knowledge, supplies, and plan. These preparation tips will help you secure your home, protect your loved ones, and respond swiftly in case of emergency.

Constant Alerts & Preparedness
During a wildfire, always stay informed through official alerts and evacuation orders via radio, mobile apps, or emergency channels. Prepare an emergency kit with essentials: water, nonperishable food, medications, important documents, communication devices, and basic tools ready for a rapid evacuation.
Home Hardening & Defensible Space
Remove combustible materials at least thirty feet from your home by clearing dry leaves, wood piles, debris. Trim tree branches to keep foliage away from structures. Choose fire-resistant landscaping and materials to minimize ignition risk and help slow wildfire spread.
Evacuation Planning & Practice
Create an evacuation plan detailing multiple escape routes and a meeting point for all household members. Practice the plan, keep vehicles fueled, and pack emergency supplies. Share details with neighbors. Monitor fire behavior and evacuate early when official orders are issued.
